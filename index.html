About Harka Sampang Rai

Harka Raj Sampang Rai, born in 1982 in Khartamchha, Khotang, is a Nepalese politician and social activist who became the mayor of Dharan Sub-Metropolitan City in 2022 as an independent candidate. With a walking stick as his electoral symbol, Rai secured 20,821 votes, defeating established political party candidates. His grassroots approach and dedication to solving Dharan’s chronic water crisis have made him a beloved figure among locals.

His Contributions:

  • Elected as Independent Mayor of Dharan (May 22, 2022): Won with 20,821 votes, using a walking stick as his symbol, defeating major party candidates.
  • Started Volunteer Water Campaign (2022–2023): Launched weekly Saturday volunteer efforts to lay water pipelines, addressing Dharan’s water crisis.
  • Kokaha River Water Project (June 13, 2023): Led a 98-day campaign to lay 42 km of pipelines, bringing water to Dharan-20.
  • World Book of Records Award (July 7, 2023): Recognized for volunteerism and water project contributions by the World Book of Records, London.
  • Sumnima-Paruhang Statue (2023–2024): Chaired a committee to build a cultural statue, raising over Rs 40 million.
  • Connected Water Sources (2022–2023): Linked Pakuwa, Sardu, Nisane, and Balu Rivers for water supply, supported by donors.
  • Canal Construction via Volunteer Campaign (June 2025): Led a 14-day volunteer campaign to build a 348-meter drainage canal in Dharan, completed for a few lakh rupees, compared to an estimated crore rupees and 6–7 months via traditional contracting. This project, highlighted in a June 29, 2025, X post, showcases Rai’s commitment to cost-effective, community-driven infrastructure development.
